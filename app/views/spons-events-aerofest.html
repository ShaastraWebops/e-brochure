SARC

Shaastra Aerial Robotics Competition (SARC) is the biggest running collegiate multirotor competition in India. The best multirotors from around the country will be put to the test through two gruelling rounds where various parameters like responsiveness, manoeuvrability, endurance will be scrutinized.If you're thinking the event is just a test of the operator then you're wrong. The rotors will be forced to think independently through performing autonomous tasks. This event is aimed at stimulating and fostering interest in this innovative technology and encouraging careers in the field, the competition challenges the students to design, fabricate and demonstrate a system capable of completing a specific and independent aerial operation. With the success this event has tasted the previous time, it is expected to be a thrilling grand finale event this year.Seeing the fastest,robust and most intelligent rotors fight it out for survival will be a visual delight for any geek!


Howar Race

From bike, cars, trucks to even plane races, we have seen everything. Or have we?Aerofest 2017 presents you a very unique race in hovercrafts. To test your hovering abilities, we challenge your bot against a variety of terrains ranging from sand terrains to water terrains to even platforms which move, this race is sure to bring out the best in town. With the clock ticking, terrains getting tougher, the one who holds their nerves and finishes the fastest wins the race. Prize money is awarded for the teams who showcased best performance.


AGT

Often asked, what is the best drone? In search of answers we bring you Aero's got talent. With the search open to all acts of drones, Aero's got talent showcases unique performances from all over India. Participants from all corners gather at Shaastra with their drones to put up a performance to impress a panel of judges. This is a celebration of creativity and talent, featuring novel designs, great applications, expert flying skills, pure crowd entertainers, and yes, the X factor. The winner gets a grand prize as well as bragging rights on what can be called the 'best drone' of them all.


Paper Plane

Right from a layman to the geekiest individual out there, this is an event for everyone. Shaastra will be introducing this event for the first time in 2017. It is expected to serve as a platform for participants to interact with the pilots hidden within themselves. The idea is massive, but the concept is devastatingly simple.They need to make a paper aeroplane and be able to either make it fly further than anyone else's, or keep it in the air for longer. A piece of uncut, untorn plain A4 paper (297x210mm) weighing no more than 100gsm is all that will be allowed; no glue, no staples, no tape. The event really is about combining design genius with their flying skill which will test the participant for a strong gut feeling and the ability to think on feet. With no technical prerequisite, it promises to be one of the largest participated event at Shaastra as it will be an event in which participants from all the age groups right from the children to their grandparents can take part .This event is guaranteed to be a crowd puller and it promises to give the participants an adrenaline rush !With two days of thought provoking and engaging competition,the event is a huge crowd puller with extensive branding opportunities.
